My Diary: The Echoes of Words

[A Conceptual Rearticulation of the Imaginary Impact of Words Through Philosophical Method]

The Spark of an Idea

One of my closest comrades quipped, "Are you an alien or what?" In that instant, a bolt of curiosity struck my mind: What form does this sentence take—interrogative or declarative? By weaving combinations from this phrase, we can craft three primary interpretations. First, the interrogative: "Are aliens real?"—probing the existence of extraterrestrial beings. Second, another interrogative: "What is an alien?"—seeking a definition of the term. And third, the declarative: "'What' itself is the alien," implying that the interrogative particle "kya" (what) is, in fact, the enigmatic entity, not a mere linguistic tool.

Building on these queries, I devised a whimsical experiment. Every question demands an answer, so if "kya" is the alien, perhaps it would respond to a signal with one of its own. Though my grasp of physics is rudimentary, I resolved to unravel this riddle through the lenses of mathematics and statistics. Let us treat "kya" as a multivariate variable, assigning it the value not of "question," "alien," or "signal," but "message." Thus, if "kya" equals "alien," upon receiving a "kya" (message), it would reciprocate with another "kya" (message)—for here, "kya" is a multifaceted entity. I wove this playful logic into the verbal tapestry above.

In essence, send a message, and a message shall return. Our assumption: "kya" is the alien. Yet the true statement lingers: "Aliens exist, what?"

The Message in the Void

I dispatched this inquiry to my dear cousins, who had visited to check on their ailing uncle—my mother's brother—though none of my streetwise friends deigned to inquire after him. The message read:

"Hey [friends—optional], it would've been great if you'd all stayed a bit longer; our conversations were left hanging... You all left, abandoning me to solitude... Missing you all—my respected, lovely, and adorable aunties, sisters, brothers, and daughters-in-law. Welcome to 13251872, Sudama Nagar, Indore. That is to say, my name is AKKCBLVYAS; I am alone in this world... Urf, your Gabbar Bhai Sahib. As Amitabh Bachchanji wisely said: 'If it aligns with your heart, it's good; if not, even better...' Heh... Heh... + Ha... Ha... Hi... Hi... He... He... Hu... Hu... Ho... Ho... Ho..."

Their reply arrived like a gentle echo:

"Wow, brother! What a wonderful message—it was quite challenging to decode. Focus on your goals, take care of Mausi and Mausaji, and yourself. All the best."

A message sent, a message received. Our assumption held: "kya" is the alien. But the original query persists: "Aliens exist, what?" Thus, if "kya" embodies the alien, the values of our multivariate—namely, question, signal, and message—could themselves form interrogatives: "What is a question?" "Did the signal arrive?" or "Did the message come?" This little study illuminates how words cascade through the human psyche, shaping thoughts in profound, unforeseen ways.

The Enigma of "Goal": Target or Circle?

As you gleaned from the message—"Focus on your goal"—I pondered: Goal as in aim or aspiration. Overjoyed, I envisioned bestowing a sweet peck on my beloved sister's cheek, for she abhors anything bitter. Yet, alas, she had already departed. That peck, a token of gratitude for her caring nudge toward my objectives. But then, a twist: "Goal" can also denote a circle. So, which goal shall I pursue? In a Facebook post, I likened myself to a dinosaur or a serpent—what if she urged focus on my egg? But eggs aren't round; they're ovoid. And science whispers that Earth itself is ovoid. Ah, the confusion: Is Earth spherical or egg-shaped? Perhaps she sought to draw my gaze earthward.

"Set your goal to become the Sun," she advised—not Surya in feminine grace, but like a scientific instrument. "Why so?" I queried. "Why should we tell?" they teased. "Why not?" I countered. "You haven't asked properly," they replied. Realization dawned: They were playfully exploiting my straightforwardness. "You are all stars, scattered like the cosmos, mothers of nature or akin to her—why withhold?" "You never bestowed that title upon us; you didn't ask." "Now, reveal it," I implored. Lifting the veil, they disclosed: "Embrace nature's rhythm; its secret is patience."

"Whose patience—yours or mine?" I pressed. "Oh, come on, that's no question at all. I read such platitudes in newspapers daily!" "That's just how we are," they chuckled. My mind, forsaking seven seas, soared to the eighth heaven and stumbled into the ninth, where I bellowed: "Hey, sky, why hide up here? Come down!" It retorted, "No, Amitji, let me stay; I know not where the bombs may fall—here I'm safe." Silence followed, so I descended and murmured to myself: "From now on, I'll never take any word in a message seriously. Let them all peck at me like birds."

Reflections from the heart: Words, like whispers from the stars, can uplift or confound. In their dance, we find both wisdom and whimsy.

— AKKCBLVYAS, Sudama Nagar, Indore
